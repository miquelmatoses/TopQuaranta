## Scope

This license covers:

- Weekly rankings (`RankingSetmanal`): positions, scores, dates, and the
  tracks / artists referenced.
- Provisional daily rankings (`RankingProvisional`).
- Daily signal data (`SenyalDiari`): playcounts, listeners, and the
  derived `score_entrada` normalization.
- Ranking algorithm coefficients and their history
  (`ConfiguracioGlobal`, `StaffAuditLog` where published at
  `/com-funciona/historial/`).

It does NOT cover:

- **Upstream data from Last.fm or Deezer** — those remain the property
  of their respective providers under their own terms. Our publication
  of derivatives falls under fair use / TDM exceptions and our own
  attribution of TopQuaranta does not transfer any rights over the
  original providers' data.
- **Artist-supplied profile content** (images, biographies, social
  links that users add). Those remain under the rights of the
  contributing artist / user.
- **The TopQuaranta name, logo, and visual identity** — trademarked
  separately; see `LICENSE` for code.

## Why CC BY

The project's cultural mission is to demonstrate that music in Catalan
is alive and measurable. Locking the data behind a restrictive license
would contradict that goal. Choosing CC BY means:

- Anyone can publish articles, make visualisations, build apps on top of
  our data without asking.
- A journalist quoting "the Top 10 in Valencian rock for April 2026"
  only needs to credit TopQuaranta.
- An academic study can cite our dataset as a primary source.

Attribution is the only ask. It ensures the origin is traceable so
readers can check our methodology and the data's freshness.

## Versioning

The license applies to the dataset as a whole, not to any particular
snapshot. A download from 2026-04-17 is redistributable under CC BY 4.0
forever, even if TopQuaranta later changes its data license — because
the CC BY grant is irrevocable for any copy already distributed.

If we ever switch licenses, that change applies to future snapshots
only, and we'll document the boundary here.
